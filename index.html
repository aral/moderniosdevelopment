The experience

You will spend three days hands‐on coding with Aral as you learn how to build native apps that conform to Apple’s coding practices and human interface guidelines.

This is also the right course for you if you have been dabbling in iOS development without entirely understanding everything you’re doing. The course will cover all of the core concepts in detail to ensure that there are no gaps left in your knowledge. You will learn why we do things in certain ways, instead of relying on copy and paste snippets. Once we’re done, nothing about Objective‐C or Cocoa Touch will seem mysterious or magical. You will be writing — and understanding the reason for — every line of code in your apps. Even past students who had already worked professionally on iOS apps and who had published apps on the App Store reported benefiting greatly from the solid foundations they gained from the course.

The aim of the workshop is not to razzle dazzle you by showing you all of the latest snazzy APIs. Instead, you will be empowered with solid foundations in Objective‐C and Cocoa Touch, an understanding of the culture and conventions of the iOS platform, and the confidence to navigate the system frameworks. Subsequently, once you’ve completed the course, you will be well‐equipped to learn and use any of the thousands of iOS APIs that you may encounter when developing your own projects.

Highlights

The following topics, among others, will be covered in this intensive, hands‐on three‐day workshop:

  • Learn about design considerations for the entire iOS platform, including hardware differences between devices (including the new iPhone 5 and new iPad) and user experience considerations when designing your apps.
  • Write professional code that is consistent with the conventions of Cocoa Touch and the system frameworks using the latest Xcode 4.x and iOS6.x releases.
  • Get a solid foundation in Objective-C and and Cocoa Touch.
  • Learn to use important C constructs like enums, structs, and typedefs.
  • Learn about classes, methods, ivars, properties, protocols, and categories.
  • Learn design patterns like Model View Controller, Observer, Mixin, etc., and how they are implemented and used in Cocoa Touch with data sources, target/action pairs, delegates, notifications, and more.
  • Learn modern practices, including using the latest collection literals (literals for arrays, dictionaries, etc.) and modern property syntax (with full understanding of what properties are syntactic sugar for).
  • Work with Automatic Reference Counting (ARC).
  • Get background on older methods of working (manual memory management, etc.) so you can work with legacy code.
  • Using class extensions to implement ‘private’ methods and properties.
  • Using blocks for asynchronous tasks.
  • Learn asynchronous networking, consume APIs, and parse JSON.
  • Create hybrid apps that use HTML, CSS, and JavaScript via the UIWebView.
  • Optimise your workflow for high resolution, including the 4" display of the new iPhone 5.
  • A look at CocoaPods and other tools for streamlining your workflow.

About the instructor

After just one day of Aral’s iOS course I’d gained a better understanding of the development tools and language than I had from many weeks referring to books and online tutorials. — John Wigzell

Aral Balkan has been developing applications professionally for over a decade and programming for close to 30 years. His iPhone app, Feathers was featured by Apple on the US App Store and was a case study in emotional design in Aarron Walter’s talks at An Event Apart and other conferences.

His iPhone/Python/Websockets hack, Grab Magic, recently won the MIPCube Hack Day in Cannes and was presented at TED@London.

Aral has been teaching iOS workshops for 3 years and developing with Objective‐C and Cocoa Touch since 2008. He also regularly teaches his iOS workshops in Oslo and Stockholm. Aral regularly gives keynotes at an eclectric array of international conferences and has spoken at, among others, MacWorld, Ùll, NSConference, Future of Web Design, and Fronteers.

Aral is a published author, contributing to numerous magazine articles and books. You can read his latest chapter from the third Smashing Magazine book for free online. It’s titled Mobile Considerations in User Experience Design: “Web or Native?”

Aral is on the board of advisors for Code Club, and this year he helped judge the Young Rewired State Festival of Code. He is scheduled to appear as a judge in the TV show Objective‐Sea in 2013.
